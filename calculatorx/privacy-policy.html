PrivacyVault Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 18, 2026

1. Overview

PrivacyVault is a local media management app. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app uses, how permissions work, and how your content is stored.

2. Data Stored on Device

PrivacyVault may store the following data on your device:

  • Your numeric passcode information
  • Album metadata such as album names, media titles, and timestamps
  • Imported or captured photos and videos
  • Encrypted thumbnails and temporary playback or export files

3. How Your Content Is Handled

  • Photos and videos you import or capture are stored inside the app container.
  • Album metadata and media files are encrypted before storage.
  • PrivacyVault does not upload your media to a remote server by default.

4. Permissions

PrivacyVault may request the following permissions:

  • Photo Library: to import photos and videos you select
  • Camera: to capture photos and videos inside the app
  • Microphone: to record audio when capturing video

These permissions are used only for the features you choose to use.

5. Temporary Files

Temporary files may be created for video playback or sharing. Depending on your settings, these files can be cleared automatically when the app locks.

6. Passcode

  • Your passcode is used to protect access to app content.
  • PrivacyVault does not provide a passcode recovery feature.
  • If you forget your passcode, access to protected content may be permanently lost.

7. Data Sharing

PrivacyVault does not sell your personal data. Content is only shared when you explicitly use export or system share features.

8. Data Deletion

You can delete albums, photos, videos, and the app itself at any time. Deleting the app removes its local container data from the device.

9. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, contact: support@privacyvault.app
